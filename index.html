→How to read this

Four guides, one path

Start wherever you are. There's no wrong entry point.

1

Start with The Cycle

If you're trying to understand what happened — or is happening — start here. The four phases, the seven stages, the brain chemistry. Why it feels the way it does.

2

Use The Anchor in crisis

If the pull to go back has arrived and you need something to hold onto right now — go straight to The Anchor. It was written for that moment.

3

Check The Undertow early

The Undertow is a self-check tool — early-warning signs that the pull is building before it peaks. Use it regularly while you're in the vulnerable period.

4

Read Clear Eyes for depth

The complete guide — all four parts, including the section on your own struggles being weaponised. If you've been told your drinking or mental health is the reason for the abuse, this one's especially for you.

Guide 01

The Cycle

For understanding what happened

The four phases. The seven stages of trauma bonding. Intermittent reinforcement and why it grips so hard. Brain chemistry, tactics, why you can't fix it — and why you were never meant to. The foundation for everything else.

Read The Cycle Guide 02

The Anchor

For when the pull comes back

Written for the moment the urge to return arrives. A truth card, a screenshot-ready reminder, the feeling vs. the fact, a protocol for if you're contacted, and a private space to write your own reasons.

Read The Anchor Guide 03

The Undertow

The self-check tool

Early-warning signs in three stages — mood, thinking, action. A checklist to help you recognise when the pull is building before it peaks, so you can interrupt the pattern before it takes hold.

Read The Undertow Guide 04

Clear Eyes

The complete guide · All four parts

Everything in one place — the cycle, the blame and your own struggles, leaving and staying gone, safety and support. Includes the section on how your drinking or mental-health struggles can be weaponised against you.
